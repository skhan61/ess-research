% ============================================================
\section{The Inference-Time Prompt Gap}
% ============================================================

\subsection{The Circular Dependency in H4}

The H4 prompt ablation (Week~1) generated box prompts as follows:

\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item Load the ground-truth binary mask for each frame.
    \item Compute the tight bounding box of all instrument pixels:
          $[x_1, y_1, x_2, y_2]$.
    \item Pass that box to SAM3 as the spatial prompt at inference.
\end{enumerate}

This procedure is circular: the bounding box is derived from the GT mask,
which is precisely the quantity the model is tasked with predicting.
The strong Dice scores reported for box-prompted experiments
(e.g., $0.9593$ on L$\to$L fold~1) are therefore \textbf{upper bounds under
an idealised condition that does not hold at deployment}.
No clinical inference pipeline has access to GT masks per frame.

\subsection{The Gap Quantified from Week 1 Data}

The performance gap between the realistic baseline (text prompt, no spatial
guidance) and the idealised upper bound (GT box) is already present in the
Week~1 results:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Prompt} & \textbf{C$\to$C} & \textbf{L$\to$L f1} &
    \textbf{L$\to$L f2} & \textbf{L$\to$L f3} \\
\midrule
Text --- realistic inference baseline (H3) & 0.9329 & 0.9049 & 0.8389 & 0.8886 \\
GT box --- idealised upper bound (H4)      & 0.9476 & 0.9593 & 0.9320 & 0.9548 \\
\midrule
\textbf{Gap} ($\Delta$Dice)                & 0.015  & 0.054  & 0.093  & 0.066  \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent The gap reaches \textbf{0.093 Dice} on the hardest session (L$\to$L fold~2).
No new experiments are required to establish this gap.
The research question for Week~3 is:

\begin{center}
\textit{Can temporal propagation close this gap without GT at inference?}
\end{center}

\section{Prior Work on Inference-Time Prompt Generation}
% ============================================================

The prompt gap is a recognised problem. Existing approaches all introduce
an \textbf{additional model or external component}:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{3.5cm} p{4cm} p{5cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Work} & \textbf{Approach} & \textbf{Limitation} \\
\midrule
Wu et al.~\cite{wu2024pointtracking} (2024) &
    External online point tracker auto-generates prompts from tracked sparse points &
    Requires a separate tracking model; manual initialisation needed \\
Surgical-DeSAM (2024) &
    DETR detection head auto-generates bounding boxes &
    Depends on detector accuracy; additional model required \\
SurgicalSAM~\cite{yue2024surgicalsam} (2024) &
    Class prototype embeddings bypass spatial prompts &
    No spatial localisation; instrument treated as a single entity \\
SAM2S (2025) &
    Enhanced memory with semantic long-term tracking &
    Requires 61k-frame surgical video dataset for training \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent \textbf{Gap in prior work:} no method uses SAM3's \textit{native} video
memory to propagate a single initial prompt across a clip --- without any
additional model, without per-frame GT, and with LoRA fine-tuning on a
small domain-specific dataset.

% ============================================================
\section{Proposed Fix: Prompt-Once Temporal Propagation}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Mechanism}

SAM3 inherits SAM2's video memory module~\cite{ravi2024sam2}. After segmenting
a frame, the model stores a memory embedding of the detected object.
On subsequent frames the memory is queried to locate and segment the same
object without any new prompt.

The proposed inference protocol is therefore:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lp{9cm}}
\toprule
\textbf{Frame} & \textbf{Action} \\
\midrule
Frame 1        & GT box prompt $\to$ SAM3 segments instrument $\to$
                 memory embedding stored \\
Frames $2 \ldots N$ & No prompt supplied. Memory queried; instrument tracked
                      and segmented. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

GT is used \textbf{once per video clip}. A single-frame initialisation of
this kind is clinically feasible --- it corresponds to one user click
identifying the instrument at clip onset.

\subsection{Distinction from Prior Work}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item No separate detector or tracker is introduced.
    \item No large-scale pre-training dataset is required.
    \item SAM3's memory mechanism is natively designed for this propagation task.
    \item The comparison to Week~1 results is direct: same model, same dataset,
          same LoRA fine-tuning --- only the prompting protocol changes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Contribution Statement}

SAM3+LoRA fine-tuned with per-frame GT box prompts achieves SOTA on
UW-Sinus-Surgery-CL; this result is unreproducible at deployment due to a
circular GT dependency. The gap between realistic and idealised performance
is quantified, and prompt-once temporal propagation using SAM3's native
video memory is proposed and evaluated as a deployment-compatible alternative.
